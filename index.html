I hold a PhD in Linguistics from the University of Lille and Leiden University in the Netherlands. I specialize in African languages, particularly Kwa languages. In recent years however, my research has focused on computational linguistics, with a special interest in applying machine learning and deep learning models to the study of African languages.

Recruited within the BANG project for this purpose, I bring my expertise in computational linguistics to enhancing our understanding of the phylogeny of Bangime. Thus, on the BANG project, I currently work on computational methods for historical and comparative linguistics of Dogon languages and Bangime, simulation of the historical trajectories of of these languages,conceiving (unsupervised) models for computational historical inference.
